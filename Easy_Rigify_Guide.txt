===============================================================================
 EASY RIGIFY - USER GUIDE
 Marker placement, AI detection & face detection
===============================================================================

Easy Rigify places the markers for a Rigify metarig, aligns and generates the
rig, and skins your mesh. This guide covers everything from installing the addon
to using the AI Body, Finger and Face detection.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 WHAT'S NEW IN THIS UPDATE
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- AI DETECT FACE (neural) - detects the core face markers automatically.
- AI Detect Body & Fingers - faster, more robust on stylised characters.
- Markers are larger and easier to grab, with blue connecting lines that show
  the layout like a skeleton.
- Live Symmetry - move a marker on one side and the other side follows.
- "Hide Markers" now hides the marker icons AND the connecting lines together.
- Arm markers stay visible after Auto Detect Body.
- One-click Install / Reinstall / Uninstall for the AI runtime (in Preferences).
- The trained AI models are encrypted and bundled with the addon.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1. REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Blender 4.2 or newer.
- The official Rigify addon enabled (Edit > Preferences > Add-ons > "Rigify").
- An internet connection ONCE, to install the AI runtime (see section 3).
- The AI detection runs on the CPU - no special GPU is required.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 2. INSTALLING THE ADDON
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Open Blender.
2. Go to  Edit > Preferences > Get Extensions  (or Add-ons).
3. Click the drop-down arrow (top-right) > "Install from Disk...".
4. Select the  easy_rigify-x.x.x.zip  file.
5. Make sure the checkbox next to "Easy Rigify" is ticked (enabled).
6. Close Preferences. Press N in the 3D Viewport to open the side panel and
   click the "AutoRig" tab.

NOTE: The three AI models ship inside the addon - you do NOT install them
separately.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 3. INSTALLING THE AI RUNTIME (ONNX RUNTIME)  -  ONE TIME
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The AI models need a small runtime (onnxruntime + Pillow) that is installed once
into Blender. This requires an internet connection.

1. Go to  Edit > Preferences > Add-ons  and expand "Easy Rigify".
2. Under "AI Detection (ONNX Runtime)" click  "Install ONNX Runtime".
3. Wait until it reports  "onnxruntime installed and ready."
   (The download is a few hundred MB - give it a minute.)
4. If it asks you to restart, close and reopen Blender.

You only do this once per Blender installation. Afterwards the button shows
"Reinstall" (to repair) and "Uninstall" (to remove the runtime).

  - No admin rights are needed; it installs into your user folder.
  - If it fails, open  Window > Toggle System Console  and try again to see the
    detailed message.

Once installed, Preferences shows:  "onnxruntime + Pillow: Ready".


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 4. THE MARKERS TAB - OVERVIEW
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Open the "AutoRig" side panel (press N) and select the "Markers" tab.

The typical order of work is:
   1) Pick your character's Body Mesh.
   2) AI Detect Body  (or Auto Detect Body).
   3) AI Detect Fingers  (then Place Fingers).
   4) Facial Markers  (AI Detect Face, or the step-by-step geometric detectors).
   5) Check the markers, adjust any by hand.
   6) Generate the rig (Rig tab).

Markers are EMPTIES. After any detection you can freely grab (G) and move any
marker to correct it. The blue lines are only a visual guide.

Tips:
   - "Show / Hide Markers" toggles all markers, their icons and the lines.
   - "Live Symmetry" (in the Mirror section) mirrors your edits left<->right.
   - "L->R" / "R->L" copy one side onto the other.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 5. AI DETECT BODY
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Detects the spine, shoulders, arms, legs and feet automatically.

1. In the Markers tab, set  "Body Mesh"  to your character's body mesh.
2. Click  "AI Detect Body".
3. Wait a few seconds - the body markers snap into place.
4. Review and nudge any marker that is off (elbows, knees and wrists are the
   most worth checking on unusual proportions).

If AI is not installed the button will ask you to install the ONNX Runtime
first (section 3). If a joint lands on the body instead of the limb, drag it
onto the limb - the rig only needs it roughly correct.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 6. AI DETECT FINGERS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Detects the finger and thumb joints for both hands.

1. Make sure the Body Mesh is set and the hands/wrists are placed.
2. Click  "AI Detect Fingers".
3. The camera briefly frames each hand while it works.
4. Review the fingertips and knuckles; drag any that drifted.
5. If needed, use  "Place Fingers"  to build the finger marker chains, and
   the HAND_TIP marker can be dragged to the middle fingertip.

Best results come from hands that are open and not clenched. Very large or very
stylised hands may need a couple of manual corrections.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 7. FACE DETECTION
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Easy Rigify offers TWO ways to place face markers. You can mix them.

A) AI DETECT FACE  (neural - recommended starting point)
   Places the core face markers in one click: lips, eyes, eyelid creases, brows,
   nose, cheeks, chin, jaw and ears. (Teeth and tongue are NOT covered by the AI
   - place those with the geometric steps below.)

   1. Open the Markers tab and expand  "Facial Markers".
   2. Tick  "Show Facial Markers".
   3. Set  "Body Mesh"  to the head (or full body) mesh.
   4. Click  "AI Detect Face".
   5. The head is framed automatically and the markers are placed on the face.
   6. Grab (G) and adjust any marker that needs it.

   Notes:
   - The "AI Detect Face" button appears only when the face model and the ONNX
     Runtime are installed.
   - It frames the HEAD automatically, so it works whether you pick just the head
     mesh or a full-body mesh.
   - If the character wears hair or a hood that hides the neck, the framing may
     be off - hide that mesh for the detection, then unhide it.

B) GEOMETRIC DETECTORS  (step by step - full control, plus teeth & tongue)
   Under "Facial Markers" you first pick the relevant meshes (eyeballs, teeth,
   tongue, eyebrows) then run the steps in order:
       Step 1 - Teeth & Tongue
       Step 2 - Brows & Eyes
       Step 3 - Lips
       Step 4 - Nose
       Step 5 - Eyelids
       Step 6 - Chin / Cheek / Jaw
       Step 7 - Forehead
   Run only the steps you need. Adjust markers by hand afterwards.

   Teeth and tongue are ALWAYS placed with these geometric steps (the AI does
   not detect them).


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 8. GENERATING THE RIG
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When the markers look correct:
   1. Switch to the "Rig" tab.
   2. Generate / align the metarig to the markers, then generate the Rigify rig.
   3. Use the "Skin" tab to bind and clean up weights.
(See the Rig and Skin tabs for the detailed buttons.)


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 9. TROUBLESHOOTING
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
"AI Detect" buttons are greyed out / ask to install
   -> Install the ONNX Runtime (section 3), then restart Blender.

"Installed, but onnxruntime is not importable yet"
   -> Restart Blender once. If it persists, open Window > Toggle System Console,
      click Install again, and read the message.

Markers show up as purple/pink blocks
   -> Fixed in this update. If you see it, reload the addon (or restart Blender)
      once.

AI Detect Face captures the whole body / frames too wide
   -> Make sure the Body Mesh is set to the character (head or full body). If a
      hair/hood mesh covers the neck, hide it for the detection.

A detected joint sits on the body instead of the limb
   -> Just drag it onto the limb; the rig only needs it approximately right.

Nothing happens when I click a detect button
   -> Confirm a Body Mesh is selected and that it is a MESH object.
