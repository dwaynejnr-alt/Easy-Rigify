# Getting Started with Easy Rigify

Welcome to Easy Rigify. This page will get you from a fresh install to a working character rig in four steps.

---

## Requirements

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- **Blender 4.2 or newer** installed
- **Rigify enabled** in Blender preferences
    - Go to Edit > Preferences > Add-ons > search "Rigify" > check the box
- A character mesh in **T-pose** (arms out, palms down, feet flat on the ground)

---

## Step 1 — Install the Add-on

1. Open Blender.
2. Go to Edit > Preferences > Get Extensions (or Add-ons).
3. Click the drop-down arrow (top right) > "Install from Disk...".
4. Select the easy_rigify-x.x.x.zip file.
5. Make sure the checkbox next to "Easy Rigify" is checked (enabled).
- Close Preferences. Press N in the 3D Viewport to open the side panel, then
click the "AutoRig" tab.

**INSTALLING THE AI RUNTIME (ONNX RUNTIME) - ONE TIME**

The AI models need a small runtime (onnxruntime + Pillow) that is installed once
into Blender. This requires an internet connection.

1. Go to Edit > Preferences > Add-ons and expand "Easy Rigify".
2. Under "AI Detection (ONNX Runtime)" click "Install ONNX Runtime".
3. Wait until it reports "onnxruntime installed and ready."
(The download is a few hundred MB - give it a minute.)
4. If it asks you to restart, close and reopen Blender.

You only do this once per Blender installation. Afterwards the button shows
"Reinstall" (to repair) and "Uninstall" (to remove the runtime).

- No admin rights are needed; it installs into your user folder.
- If it fails, open Window > Toggle System Console and try again to see the
detailed message.

Once installed, Preferences shows:  "onnxruntime + Pillow: Ready".

---

## Step 2 — Place Markers

Markers are the landmarks Easy Rigify uses to position your rig bones.

1. Select your character mesh in the viewport
2. Open the **Markers** tab in the Easy Rigify panel
3. Click **Auto Detect Body** — markers will snap to joint centers across your mesh
4. If your character has a face rig, click **Detect Face Landmarks** to place facial markers
5. Check the viewport — each marker shows a color-coded icon above it
    - Green = body joints
    - Orange = face (brows, nose, lips)
    - Blue = eye and eyelid markers
    - Yellow = teeth and tongue
    - Purple = cheeks, chin, ears
6. Run **Check All Markers** to confirm no markers are missing or overlapping before continuing

> If any marker landed in the wrong place, select it and move it manually in the viewport like any other object.
> 

---

## Step 3 — Generate and Align the Rig

1. Switch to the **Rig** tab
2. Click **Add Metarig (With Face)** or **Add Metarig (No Face)** depending on your character
3. Click **Align Rig** — every bone moves to match your marker positions
4. Click **Generate Rig** — Rigify builds the final control rig

---

## Step 4 — Bind Your Mesh

1. Switch to the **Skin** tab
2. Click **Detect Meshes** — Easy Rigify finds your body, clothing, eyes, and teeth meshes automatically
3. Click **Smart Bind** — each mesh is attached to the rig with appropriate starting weights
4. Use the **Surgical Weight Fix** presets to clean up any joint bleeding
5. Use **Symmetrize Weights** if one side looks different from the other

---

## You're Done

Your character now has a fully functional Rigify rig with weights applied. From here you can:

- Fine-tune weights in Weight Paint mode using the tools in the **Skin** tab
- Add fan bones for better joint volume from the **Tools** tab
- Pose and animate your character using the Rigify control rig

---

## Need Help?

Each tab in the panel has a **Help button** (top right) that opens an online guide for that section.

For bug reports, go to **Edit > Preferences > Add-ons > Easy Rigify** and click **Report Bug** — it will open a pre-filled email to the developer.
