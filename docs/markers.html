Placing Markers

Markers are the foundation of Easy Rigify. They tell the addon where each joint on your character should be. You can place them automatically with detection, or manually for complete control.

Body Markers

Body markers define the spine, arms, legs, and feet of your character.

Auto-Detect (Geometric)

  • Works on any humanoid shape
  • Fast and reliable
  • Reads the mesh geometry

EasyDetect (AI) - Full Edition

  • Uses trained neural network
  • Better on unusual proportions
  • Handles stylized characters

Manual Placement

Click the "Place Body Markers" button and follow the on-screen hints. Each marker lights up when selected.

💡 Tip: If auto-detection isn't quite right, grab a marker (press G) and nudge it into place. The addon will snap it to the nearest relevant geometry.

Finger Markers

Fingers can be placed in one click with AI detection, or manually with a fast two-click workflow.

One-Click Detection (AI) - Full Edition

Click "EasyDetect Fingers" and all hand and finger joints are placed automatically.

Two-Click Per Finger (Manual)

  1. Click "Place Fingers"
  2. For each finger: First click = tip, Second click = knuckle
  3. Middle joints are interpolated automatically
  4. The right hand mirrors from the left

Per-Finger Picker

Need to redo just the thumb? Use the per-finger picker to place individual fingers without redoing the whole hand.

ℹ️ Note: Fingers are detected independently from the wrist marker, so the wrist can be adjusted without affecting finger placement.

Face Markers

Face markers detect brows, eyes, eyelids, lips, nose, cheeks, chin, jaw, and forehead.

One-Click Detection (AI) - Full Edition

Click "EasyDetect Face" to automatically place all facial markers.

Section-by-Section (Manual)

Detect each facial region separately for more control:

  • Eyes — Requires separate eye mesh objects
  • Brows — Requires separate eyebrow mesh
  • Lips — Requires teeth and tongue meshes
  • Nose & Cheeks — Automatically detected from head
  • Chin & Jaw — Deform bone placement
⚠️ Setup Note: For best results, set up separate objects for:
  • Eyes (eyeball geometry)
  • Eyebrows (separate mesh)
  • Teeth (separate mesh)
  • Tongue (separate mesh)
If you don't have these, the detection will use fallbacks.

Editing Markers

Move a Marker

  • Press G to grab/move
  • Move your mouse to reposition
  • Press X, Y, or Z to constrain to an axis
  • Click to confirm

Show/Hide Markers

Toggle "Show / Hide Markers" in the Markers panel to see or hide all markers and their connecting lines.

Validate Markers

Click "Check All Markers" to verify:

  • All required markers are placed
  • No markers are overlapping
  • No markers are outside your mesh

Live Symmetry & Mirroring

Mirror Markers

Copy all markers from left to right (or vice versa) in one click.

  • Click "Mirror L→R" to copy left side to right
  • Click "Mirror R→L" to copy right side to left

Live Symmetry

Enable "Live Symmetry" to edit one side and see changes mirror in real time across your character's symmetry plane.

💡 Tip: Live Symmetry is great for fine-tuning both sides simultaneously. Disable it for asymmetrical characters.

Best Practices

  • Start in a clean pose: T-pose or A-pose with applied transforms
  • Auto-detect first: Use detection, then adjust manually if needed
  • Check markers before rigging: Bad marker placement = bad rig alignment
  • Marker size: Markers scale themselves to your character; they should look "right" proportionally
  • Use Live Symmetry: Faster than manual mirroring for bilateral editing
