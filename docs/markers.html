Markers

Markers tell the addon where each joint on your character should be. Place them automatically with detection, or manually for full control.

Body Markers

Body markers define the spine, arms, legs, and feet.

Auto Detect (geometric)

Reads the mesh geometry directly. Works on any humanoid shape, fast and deterministic. Available in both editions.

EasyDetect (AI, Full Edition)

Uses a trained neural network. More accurate on unusual proportions and stylized characters.

Manual placement

Click "Place Body Markers" and follow the on-screen hints. The selected marker is highlighted.

If auto-detection isn't quite right, grab a marker (G) and nudge it into place.

Finger Markers

One-click detection (AI, Full Edition)

Click "EasyDetect Fingers" — all hand and finger joints are placed automatically.

Two-click per finger (manual)

  1. Click "Place Fingers"
  2. For each finger: first click = tip, second click = knuckle
  3. Middle joints are interpolated
  4. The right hand mirrors from the left

Per-finger picker

Redo a single finger (e.g. the thumb) without redoing the whole hand.

Fingers are detected independently from the wrist marker — adjusting the wrist doesn't affect finger placement.

Face Markers

Covers brows, eyes, eyelids, lips, nose, cheeks, chin, jaw, and forehead.

One-click detection (AI, Full Edition)

Click "EasyDetect Face" to place all facial markers at once.

Section-by-section (manual)

  • Eyes — needs a separate eye mesh object
  • Brows — needs a separate eyebrow mesh
  • Lips — needs teeth and tongue meshes
  • Nose & cheeks — detected from the head mesh
  • Chin & jaw — deform bone placement
For best results, set up separate objects for eyes, eyebrows, teeth, and tongue. Without them, detection falls back to estimates.

Editing Markers

  • Press G to grab/move a marker, X/Y/Z to constrain to an axis
  • Toggle "Show / Hide Markers" to hide markers and connecting lines together
  • Click "Check All Markers" to confirm every required marker is placed, and flag anything overlapping or outside the mesh

Mirroring & Live Symmetry

"Mirror L→R" / "Mirror R→L" copies all markers from one side to the other in one click.

"Live Symmetry" mirrors edits in real time — move a marker on one side and its counterpart moves with it across the character's symmetry plane.

Disable Live Symmetry for asymmetrical characters.
