Rigging Workflow

Once your markers are placed, the rigging process happens in two steps: align the Rigify metarig to your markers, then generate the final control rig.

Overview

The Rigify metarig is an armature that defines the structure of your rig. Easy Rigify automatically positions every bone in this metarig to match your character, then Rigify generates the final control rig with IK, FK, and other features.

ℹ️ What's a metarig? It's a special armature that Rigify reads to generate your control rig. You don't animate with it—Rigify creates the actual control rig from it.

Align Rig to Markers

The Process

  1. Make sure all markers are placed and validated
  2. Go to the Rig tab in Easy Rigify
  3. Click "Align Rig to Markers"
  4. Easy Rigify will:
    • Create a Rigify metarig if one doesn't exist
    • Position every bone to match your markers
    • Calculate correct bone rolls from your mesh surface

Bone Rolls

Bone rolls define the rotation direction of each bone (which way is "up" along the bone). Easy Rigify calculates these automatically based on your mesh surface, so deformation is smooth.

Generate Rig

Creating Your Control Rig

  1. After alignment, click "Generate Rig"
  2. Rigify reads the metarig and generates:
    • Control bones (for animation)
    • Deform bones (connected to mesh weights)
    • Mechanism bones (IK poles, hand IK, etc.)
  3. A new armature called "rig" is created
✅ Your rig is ready! You can now bind your mesh and start animating.

Regenerate Without Losing Work

Saving & Restoring Custom Changes

Need to tweak the metarig and regenerate? Easy Rigify can backup and restore your custom changes.

Backup Before Changes

  1. Click "Backup Custom Rig Changes"
  2. This saves any custom control shapes, bone properties, or modifications

Tweak & Regenerate

  1. Make adjustments to the metarig in Edit Mode
  2. Click "Generate Rig" again

Restore Your Changes

  1. Click "Restore Custom Rig Changes"
  2. Your custom shapes and properties are reapplied to the new rig
💡 Tip: This workflow is great for iterating on rig geometry or control shapes without starting over.

Troubleshooting Alignment

Misaligned Bones

Cause: Markers are placed incorrectly or unapplied scale/rotation on your mesh.

Solution:

  • Apply all transforms on your mesh: Object → Apply → All Transforms
  • Re-place markers and validate with "Check All Markers"
  • Run "Align Rig to Markers" again

Bad Bone Rolls

Cause: Deformation looks twisted or strange.

Solution:

  • Check that your mesh normal directions are correct (mesh isn't inverted)
  • Make sure markers are placed symmetrically on left and right sides
  • Re-align and regenerate

Rig Doesn't Appear

Cause: Metarig wasn't created or Rigify failed to generate.

Solution:

  • Make sure Rigify is enabled in Blender preferences
  • Check the system console (Help → Toggle System Console) for error messages
  • Try deleting the metarig and running "Align Rig to Markers" fresh

Understanding Bone Rolls (Advanced)

Bone rolls define the rotation axis of each bone. In Blender, you'll see this as the angle of the bone in pose mode.

Why Bone Rolls Matter

  • Affect deformation direction in pose mode
  • Influence IK solver behavior (elbow point)
  • Determine how weights deform around joints

Easy Rigify's Automatic Calculation

When you align the rig, Easy Rigify calculates bone rolls by:

  1. Reading your mesh surface normals
  2. Determining the local "forward" direction for each bone
  3. Setting the roll so deformation follows your mesh shape
💡 Manual Adjustment: If a bone's roll is off, you can adjust it manually in Edit Mode by rotating the bone around its length axis.
