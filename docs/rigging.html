Rig

Once markers are placed, align the Rigify metarig to them, then generate the final control rig.

Overview

The metarig is an armature Rigify reads to generate your control rig. You don't animate with it directly — Rigify builds the actual control rig from it, with IK, FK, and other mechanisms.

Align Rig to Markers

  1. Make sure markers are placed and validated
  2. Go to the Rig tab
  3. Click "Align Rig to Markers"

This creates a metarig if one doesn't exist, positions every bone to match your markers, and calculates bone rolls from the mesh surface.

Generate Rig

Click "Generate Rig" after alignment. Rigify reads the metarig and produces control bones (for animation), deform bones (connected to mesh weights), and mechanism bones (IK poles, hand IK, etc.) in a new armature called "rig".

Regenerate Without Losing Work

Back up and restore custom changes so you can tweak the metarig and regenerate without starting over.

  1. Click "Backup Custom Rig Changes" before editing
  2. Adjust the metarig in Edit Mode
  3. Click "Generate Rig" again
  4. Click "Restore Custom Rig Changes" to reapply your custom shapes and properties

Troubleshooting Alignment

Misaligned bones

Usually unapplied scale/rotation on the mesh. Apply all transforms, re-validate markers with "Check All Markers", then re-run "Align Rig to Markers".

Bad bone rolls

Check that mesh normals aren't inverted and that markers are placed symmetrically, then re-align and regenerate.

Rig doesn't appear

Make sure Rigify is enabled in Blender preferences. Check the system console (Help → Toggle System Console) for errors, or delete the metarig and re-run alignment.

Bone Rolls

Bone roll defines the rotation axis of a bone and affects deformation direction and IK solver behavior (e.g. elbow pole direction).

Easy Rigify calculates rolls automatically by reading mesh surface normals near each bone and setting the roll so deformation follows your mesh shape. You can adjust a bone's roll manually in Edit Mode if needed.
