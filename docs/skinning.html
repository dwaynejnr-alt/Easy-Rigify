Skin

Skinning binds your mesh to the rig and controls how it deforms as bones move.

Automatic Binding

  1. Select your character mesh
  2. Go to the Skin tab
  3. Click "Bind Mesh"

This creates an Armature modifier and initializes vertex weights based on bone proximity.

Weight Cleanup

Automatic binding is a good starting point, but joints like shoulders, elbows, wrists, and knees usually need refinement.

Tighten Weights

  1. Click "Tighten Weights"
  2. Choose a preset: Gentle, Default, or Aggressive
  3. Select regions to process (fingers, forearms, upper arms, shoulders, shins, thighs, spine, neck)
  4. Click "Tighten"
Start with Default. For stylized rigs, try Gentle; for exaggerated proportions, Aggressive.

Common Deformation Issues

Shoulder collapse

Mesh sinks in when the arm is raised. Tighten Weights on the Shoulder region, or manually reduce upper-arm influence near the shoulder.

Elbow pinching

Forearm creases too sharply. Tighten Weights on Forearms, or manually smooth the transition between forearm and upper-arm weights.

Finger deformation

Fingers twist or collapse when bent. Tighten Weights on Fingers, then Smooth Weights to blend transitions.

Joint bleed

Nearby geometry deforms when it shouldn't. Run "Analyze Joint Bleed" to find problem areas, then "Repair Joint Bleed" to fix them.

Weight Tools

  • Smooth Weights — blends weight transitions to reduce hard edges
  • Mirror Weights — copies weights left↔right
  • Prune Weights — removes negligible weight values below a threshold
  • Enter / Exit Weight Paint — quick toggle into and out of paint mode

Manual Weight Painting

Automated tools handle most cases, but unusual proportions or stylized deformation sometimes need hand-tuning.

  1. Click "Enter Weight Paint" in the Tools tab
  2. Select a bone from the Outliner or armature
  3. Paint to increase or decrease influence on that bone
  4. Click "Exit Weight Paint" when done
Hold Shift to invert the brush (decrease weights). Use [ / ] to resize the brush.

Testing the Rig

Select the armature, press Tab for Pose Mode, and test a few positions: T-pose, bent elbows, bent knees, shoulder shrug, closed fist. Exit Pose Mode, adjust weights, and re-test until deformation looks right.
